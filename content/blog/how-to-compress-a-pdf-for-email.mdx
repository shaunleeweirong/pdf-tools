To compress a PDF for email, open a free compress tool, drop your file in, and download the smaller version — it usually takes a few seconds and, with an in-browser tool, your file is never uploaded anywhere. Most email providers reject attachments larger than 20–25 MB, and a scan- or photo-heavy PDF blows past that fast. Shrinking the file is almost always quicker than fighting your email client.

## The fastest way to compress a PDF for email

1. Open the [free Compress PDF tool](/compress-pdf).
2. Drop your PDF into the box, or click to browse for it.
3. Click **Compress PDF** and wait a moment while it shrinks in your browser.
4. Download the smaller file and attach it to your email.

Because the tool runs entirely in your browser, the document never leaves your device — handy when the PDF is a contract, invoice, or anything you would rather not upload to a stranger's server.

## Why is my PDF so large?

Almost always, it is the images. A PDF made from phone photos or a scanner stores full-resolution pictures on every page, and those add up quickly. Text-only PDFs are tiny by comparison. That is why compression helps most on image-heavy files — and barely changes a plain text document.

## What if it is still too big after compressing?

If one pass is not enough, you have two easy options:

- **Split it into parts.** Send the document as two or three smaller PDFs with the [Split PDF tool](/split-pdf), then recombine later if needed.
- **Send only the pages that matter.** Extract the specific pages the recipient actually needs instead of the whole file.

If you are trying to *combine* several files into one before sending, do that first with the [Merge PDF tool](/merge-pdf), then compress the result.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the maximum PDF size for email?

Gmail and Outlook both cap attachments around 20–25 MB. If your file is larger, either compress it or send a link instead.

### Does compressing a PDF reduce quality?

It can slightly reduce image sharpness, since compression works mainly by shrinking images. For everyday documents the difference is usually invisible; for print-quality artwork, keep an original copy.

### Is it safe to compress a confidential PDF online?

With an in-browser tool like this one, yes — the file is processed on your own device and never uploaded, so nothing is stored on a server.
